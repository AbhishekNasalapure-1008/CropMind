Reference Database

NPK Nutrient Guide

Understanding what each nutrient does and how to identify its deficiency.

N

Nitrogen Essential for vegetative growth & chlorophyll

Visual Symptoms
  • General yellowing (chlorosis) from older leaves
  • Pale green to yellow uniform discoloration
  • Stunted growth and thin, weak stems
  • Reduced tillering in cereals
Most Affected Crops
Maize Rice Wheat Sugarcane Tomato
Common Fixes
  • Urea (46-0-0) — soil or foliar
  • Ammonium nitrate for severe cases
  • Green manure / cover crops long-term
P

Phosphorus Critical for roots, flowers & energy transfer

Visual Symptoms
  • Purple/reddish tint on leaf undersides & stems
  • Dark green, dull appearance of leaves
  • Delayed flowering and maturity
  • Poor root development and water uptake
Most Affected Crops
Soybean Potato Wheat Cotton Legumes
Common Fixes
  • DAP (18-46-0) for fast correction
  • TSP (0-46-0) for severe deficiency
  • Maintain pH 6.0–7.0 for availability
K

Potassium Regulates water, enzymes & stress tolerance

Visual Symptoms
  • Marginal leaf scorch (brown, crispy edges)
  • Interveinal chlorosis on older leaves
  • Weak stems prone to lodging
  • Poor fruit quality and reduced sugar content
Most Affected Crops
Tomato Potato Sugarcane Cotton Banana
Common Fixes
  • MOP (0-0-60) for general correction
  • SOP (0-0-50) for chloride-sensitive crops
  • Potassium nitrate via fertigation
